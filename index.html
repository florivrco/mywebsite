Jeddah Public Transport Program (JPTP)

Civil Engineer involved in modernizing Jeddah's transportation system, focusing on urban mobility and smart transit solutions.

Project Overview

As part of the national initiative to modernize Jeddah's transportation system, the JPTP aims to transform the city into a more sustainable, accessible, and interconnected urban environment. I was directly involved in a major phase of the project, focusing on the largest and most populated district in Jeddah, where traffic congestion was a critical challenge.

Key Contributions

  • Focused Urban Strategy: Led the development and adaptation of transport infrastructure specifically tailored for the largest residential district in Jeddah, addressing high-density traffic areas with long-standing congestion issues.
  • Multi-Modal Transit Hubs: Designed and supported the implementation of intermodal "pulse stations" that integrated public and private transportation modes—combing smart scooters, private car parking, shuttle buses, and BRT access in a single location. This approach reduced car dependency and increased accessibility for residents.
  • Smart Traffic Decongestion: Introduced strategic infrastructure solutions such as dedicated BRT lanes, adaptive signal controls, and optimized pedestrian crossings to facilitate continuous flow and reduce bottlenecks in key corridors.
  • Transit-Oriented Development (TOD): Supported land use coordination and path re-alignment to ensure connectivity between residential areas, commercial centers, and public transit stations, promoting walkability and smart mobility access.
  • Innovative Mobility Tools: Contributed to the planning and spatial analysis using GIS-based simulations and Civil 3D to optimize traffic distribution and enhance modal shift efficiency.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Collaborated with city planners, public transport authorities, and urban design consultants to ensure that the solutions were feasible, inclusive, and aligned with long-term mobility goals.

Impact Achieved

  • Reduced traffic load by offering seamless modal shifts through integrated transport hubs.
  • Improved accessibility and reduced average commute times within the district by a measurable margin.
  • Enhanced the livability and mobility standards of one of Jeddah's most densely populated areas.
  • Contributed to a scalable model that can be replicated in other congested zones across the city.
